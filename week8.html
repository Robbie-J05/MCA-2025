Analysis


Waveform analysis can be good to show the volume intensity of a piece. It makes it easy to recognise parts that might be too loud or quiet and can show fade in and fade outs. What it lacks is a recognisable pattern in a musical sense. Any audio can have peaks without necessarily needing to be "music". Time-frequency analysis allows us to recognise the pattern of notes and pitches in the piece that can identify the key, range or common themes in the piece. We see in great gig in the sky there is a clear change in the music from the intro to the main section, in both images. But in the song there is a clear guitar bend that takes us into that section that we can see as a small line going up in the spectrogram.
